1. What is Heat?

Heat is a form of energy that makes an object hot. Heat flows from a hotter body to a colder body.

Examples: Sun heats Earth, stove heats water.

2. Temperature

Temperature measures the degree of hotness or coldness of a body.

SI Unit: Kelvin (K)

Other Units: Celsius (°C), Fahrenheit (°F)

3. Difference Between Heat and Temperature

Heat is energy. Temperature measures hotness.

Heat flows from hot to cold. Temperature does not flow.

4. Clinical Thermometer

Used to measure body temperature.

Range: 35°C to 42°C

Has bulb, mercury, scale and kink.

5. Laboratory Thermometer

Used in experiments.

Range: -10°C to 110°C

No kink present.

6. Precautions While Using Thermometer

  • Wash before and after use.
  • Read at eye level.
  • Handle carefully.
  • Shake mercury down before use.

7. Modes of Transfer of Heat

There are three modes:

  • Conduction
  • Convection
  • Radiation

8. Conduction

Transfer of heat in solids without movement of particles.

Example: Metal spoon becomes hot in tea.

9. Convection

Transfer of heat in liquids and gases by movement of particles.

Example: Boiling water.

10. Radiation

Transfer of heat without medium.

Example: Heat from Sun reaches Earth.

11. Conductors

Materials that allow heat to pass easily.

Examples: Copper, Iron, Aluminium.

12. Insulators

Materials that do not allow heat to pass easily.

Examples: Wood, Plastic, Rubber.

13. Expansion of Solids

Solids expand on heating.

Example: Railway tracks have gaps.

14. Expansion of Liquids

Liquids expand more than solids.

Example: Mercury rises in thermometer.

15. Expansion of Gases

Gases expand the most.

Example: Hot air balloon rises.

16. Sea Breeze

During day, land heats faster. Cool air moves from sea to land.

17. Land Breeze

During night, land cools faster. Cool air moves from land to sea.

18. Dark and Light Colours

Black absorbs more heat. White reflects heat.

19. Normal Body Temperature

Normal body temperature is 37°C or 98.6°F.

20. Importance of Heat

Heat is used in cooking, industries, electricity generation, and daily life.
