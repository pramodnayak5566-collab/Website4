Class 7 CBSE Science

Chapter 4: Heat

20 Long Questions and Answers

1. What is heat? Explain with examples.

Heat is a form of energy that makes an object hot. It flows from a hotter body to a colder body. Example: Sun heats Earth, stove heats water.

2. Differentiate between heat and temperature.

Heat is a form of energy while temperature measures hotness. Heat flows from hot to cold, temperature does not flow.

3. Explain the structure and working of a clinical thermometer.

A clinical thermometer has a bulb, mercury, scale and kink. Mercury expands due to body heat and rises in the tube.

4. Write precautions while using a clinical thermometer.

Wash before and after use, read at eye level, shake mercury down, handle carefully.

5. Explain conduction with examples.

Conduction is transfer of heat in solids without movement of particles. Example: Metal spoon becomes hot in tea.

6. Explain convection with examples.

Convection is transfer of heat in liquids and gases through movement of particles. Example: Boiling water, sea breeze.

7. Explain radiation with examples.

Radiation is transfer of heat without any medium. Example: Heat from Sun reaches Earth.

8. What are conductors and insulators?

Conductors allow heat to pass easily (Copper). Insulators do not allow heat to pass easily (Wood).

9. Explain expansion of solids on heating.

Solids expand when heated. Example: Railway tracks have gaps.

10. Explain expansion of liquids and gases.

Liquids expand more than solids. Gases expand the most. Example: Hot air balloon rises.

11. Explain sea breeze.

During day, land heats faster. Warm air rises and cool air from sea moves to land.

12. Explain land breeze.

During night, land cools faster. Cool air moves from land to sea.

13. Why do we wear light-coloured clothes in summer?

Light colours reflect heat and keep us cool.

14. Why are cooking utensils made of metals but handles of plastic?

Metals conduct heat for cooking. Plastic handles prevent burning.

15. What is importance of heat in daily life?

Used in cooking, heating, electricity generation, industries.

16. What is maximum–minimum thermometer?

It measures highest and lowest temperature of the day. Used in weather stations.

17. Describe laboratory thermometer.

Used in experiments. Range: -10°C to 110°C. No kink present.

18. Why are gaps left in railway tracks?

To allow expansion during summer and prevent bending.

19. What happens when two objects of different temperatures touch?

Heat flows from hotter object to colder object until both reach same temperature.

20. Explain working of hot air balloon.

Hot air expands and becomes lighter, so it rises and lifts the balloon.
